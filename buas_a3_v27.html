Can AI-based forecasting reduce empty container movements?

Hypothesis

"Applying an AI-based demand forecasting model to historical shipment data reduces the number of unplanned empty container repositioning trips compared to planning without forecasting."

"What if we could predict this before it happens?"

This research explores how AI-based forecasting reduces empty container movements using a simulation on real industry data.

Motivation

This research is motivated by a clear gap between vision and practice. While the Physical Internet establishes a framework for intelligent logistics built on shared data, the application of AI forecasting as a concrete step toward that vision remains untested in the context of empty container management.

Objective

To simulate the impact of AI-based forecasting on empty container movements using real industry shipment data, measuring the reduction in unplanned repositioning trips compared to planning without forecasting, with the aim of demonstrating its operational value for logistics companies.

Key insights

In 2024, empty container volumes grew faster than loaded ones, meaning more ships are moving with nothing inside than ever before (Eurostat, 2024).

Every time a container is moved empty, the shipping company pays the full transport cost but earns nothing in return, making it one of the biggest financial losses in the industry (Xu et al., 2024).

The most critical AI solutions in ports rely on machine learning techniques, showing that AI is no longer experimental but an industry-ready tool (Farzadmehr et al., 2023).
